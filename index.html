Rainy is the BEST

To me, my feelings about rainy seasons are a bit complicated. It's like a love-hate relationship. I was born in Bangkok, Thailand, where it's summer almost all the time, and I really dislike the hot and sweaty weather. That's why I have a bit of a love-hate thing with summer. Rainy seasons, on the other hand, give me a break from all that sweating and provide a fresh and cool change. Plus, there's something special about the sound and smell of rain that you can't find in any other season. I've listed five reasons why I love rainy seasons, and even though I'm aware of the downsides, my love for them still wins out.

Pros of the rainy

  1. Renewal of Life: Rainy seasons bring a fresh start to all forms of life. The rain nourishes plants and replenishes water sources, contributing to the growth of lush greenery and a rejuvenated ecosystem.
  2. Air Quality Improvement: Rain helps to cleanse the air by removing dust and pollutants. It washes away particles from the atmosphere, leading to improved air quality and better breathing conditions.
  3. Diverse Landscapes: Rainy seasons transform landscapes, whether you're by the beach or in the mountains. The added moisture creates vibrant and picturesque scenes that are a departure from the usual dry conditions.
  4. Observing Wind Patterns: Rainy seasons often come with winds. It's an excellent opportunity to observe and appreciate the patterns and strength of the wind, adding an element of wonder to the season.
  5. Safe and Peaceful Environment: Rainy seasons allow humans to stay comfortably at home. This can be seen as the most secure and tranquil time of the year, as you can avoid extreme weather conditions and enjoy the coziness of your home.
Rainy seasons, despite occasional inconveniences, stand out as some of the best seasons globally. They usher in a renewal of life, nourishing plants and rejuvenating ecosystems. Moreover, they purify the air, cleansing it of dust and pollutants, contributing to improved overall health. Rainy seasons transform landscapes into vibrant, picturesque scenes, breaking the monotony of dry conditions. This natural beauty and environmental revitalization, combined with the soothing sound of raindrops, create a sense of tranquility and wonder that makes rainy seasons truly special.

Beyond the Surface: A Closer Look at its Advantages, Risks, and Emotional Impact

It is true that there are many advantages to the rainy season, but it is not enough to break even the disadvantages of it. There are several things to consider and be afraid of in the rainy season such as power outages, transportation delays, or even floods because nature is unpredictable. Not only those things, rainy days will also give a bad mood and vibes for several people that have special issues. Depression and Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) will likely be triggered because of the weather. So I have a strong belief that rainy weather is not as good as it seems and cannot be considered the best season.

Cons of the rainy

  1. Depression and Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD): For some individuals, prolonged periods of rain and gloomy weather can lead to feelings of depression and SAD. Reduced exposure to sunlight can affect mood and overall well-being.
  2. Power Outages: Storms often accompany rainy seasons, which can result in power outages due to lightning strikes or flooding of electrical infrastructure. This can disrupt daily life and business operations.
  3. Transportation Delays: Heavy rain can lead to transportation delays, including flight cancellations, road closures, and public transit disruptions. This can inconvenience commuters and travelers.
  4. Disruption of Outdoor Activities: Outdoor events, sports, and recreational activities can be hampered during rainy seasons due to wet conditions. This can limit opportunities for outdoor enjoyment.
  5. Flooding and Waterlogging: Excessive rainfall can lead to flooding and waterlogging, causing damage to homes, infrastructure, and agricultural fields. It can disrupt daily life and result in property losses.

The snowy landscape appears romantic and stunning, but winter in Canada brings numerous challenges. This vast country presents difficulties not only for interpersonal communication and outdoor activities constrained by the cold weather but also for transportation methods like cars and associated maintenance expenses, to meet anyone you want to meet.

Cons of the winter

Even though there are companies that clean up the snow, it is not 100% gone and will still leave residues. This makes drivers have to be more cautious because there will be more likely to be accident due to the slippery road.

There are specific tires that need to be adjusted to the vehicle for winter and safety purposes. This will be a hassle everytime winter comes, and is going to cost more than other seasons.
